Fairy Tale Route: Best Travel Months

Fairy Tale Travel Tips

1. Follow the Breadcrumb Trail

Explore the Sababurg Castle ruins deep in Reinhardswald Forest — believed to inspire Hansel & Gretel’s tale. Bring snacks, just don’t drop them.

Visit Sababurg Castle →

2. Sleep Like Sleeping Beauty

Book a stay at Sababurg’s fairy tale hotel where wild roses still climb the castle walls — the perfect place to dream for 100 years (or a weekend).

Stay at the Fairy Tale Hotel →

3. Mirror, Mirror in the Town

In Lohr am Main, the Snow White Museum claims to have the original “talking mirror” from the Brothers Grimm tale. Who’s the fairest of them all?

Visit the Snow White Museum →

4. Tower Over the Town Like Rapunzel

Climb the tower at Trendelburg where Rapunzel was said to let down her hair. You can even stay overnight in the tower suite!

See Rapunzel’s Tower →

5. Don’t Talk to Wolves

The Schwalm Region — home of Red Riding Hood — features red-cap folk costumes and deep woods where “the wolf” still roams (on signage, don’t worry).

Explore the Red Riding Hood Trail →

6. Make a Wish at Mother Holle’s Spring

At Hoher Meißner Mountain, toss a coin into Mother Holle’s spring and make a wish. Just don’t fluff her featherbed unless you want snow.

Visit Mother Holle’s Mountain →

7. Dance Through the Princess Pavilion

Bad Wildungen hosts fairy tale events and castles that echo “The Twelve Dancing Princesses.” Some say the floorboards still creak at midnight.

Explore Bad Wildungen →

8. Hunt for the Golden Bird

Stroll the golden courtyards of Schloss Philippsruhe near Hanau. Some say this royal garden inspired “The Golden Bird.”

Visit Schloss Philippsruhe →

9. Discover Rumpelstiltskin’s Mill

Near Marburg, follow signs to old water mills and weaving huts — the kind Rumpelstiltskin might’ve haunted. Spin straw into stories instead of gold.

Explore Marburg Region →

10. Wander the Enchanted Forest by Lantern

Join a twilight tour through Reinhardswald — Germany’s most legendary forest. It’s darker, quieter, and more magical than you’d expect.

Book a Night Walk →

11. Visit the Brothers Grimm House

In Steinau an der Straße, the Brothers Grimm Museum offers artifacts, manuscripts, and story origins. It's the ultimate fairy tale pilgrimage site.

Enter Grimm World →

12. Stroll in Rauschenberg Village

Wander through misty woodlands to reach the historic town of Rauschenberg Village — a perfect real-world stand-in for many of the towns echoed in the Grimms' Brothers fairytales.

Explore Rauschenberg Village →
